Why are you laughing?

2024 25 future theatre teachers trained in healthy boundaries of humour
15 emerging artists trained in inclusive comedy practices
1 video essay on "humour as conflict"

A project that challenges the stereotypes and discrimination rooted in Romanian comedy.

At its center stands "The Death and Life of Bulă", a show that reimagines the fate of Romania's most iconic and problematic comic figure, questioning what humour leaves behind and what it can create instead.

Around it, a series of workshops with stand-up comedians, students, teenagers and an inclusive stand-up contest, explore the fine line between laughter as a tool for connection and laughter as a weapon of exclusion.

Through stage and dialogue alike, we aimed to ask what comedy means today and how it can grow into something more inclusive, empathetic, and alive.
